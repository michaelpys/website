If you have any of the following symptoms, the headache you have is most likely caused by the neck, even if you don't have neck pain, so keep reading. This website is especially for you.


  • Headache is one-sided headache, which, for example occurs on the left, and the next episode on the right, that is, it swaps sides between episodes (or within the same episode).
  • Headache on both sides of the head at the same time, but is stronger on the left and other times on the right, this confirms that the neck is responsible for migraine headache.
  • Headache starting in the neck and/or headaches that go along with neck discomfort or stiffness.
  • One-sided headache or headache on both sides but stronger on the one (always the same) side.
  • Headache triggered by sustained postures, for example neck forward.
  • Ongoing headache (4 weeks) after head and/or neck injury.
  • Headache that is gradually becoming more frequent.

Even if your headache symptoms aren't listed above, there is a very high chance the treatment suggestions can be very helpful to you.
